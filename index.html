The Truth about the Trace

Very likely that this is true
All recorded uses of the Trace in the Harry Potter Books

Mad-Eye Moody says "the trace detects magical activity around under-seventeens" (Hallows).

Dumbledore says that the Trace detects where the magic is coming from, but not who performed it (Prince).

Nymphadora Tonks performs spells like "Scourgify" and "Locomotor Trunk" after Harry has been expelled from Hogwarts (Order).

Mad-Eye Moody performs a disillusionment charm on Harry then too (Order).

Dumbledore conjures up a chair and offers the Dursleys floating magical mead (Prince).

Mad-Eye and Tonks was/is a ministry official (Auror) and Dumbledore is the chief warlock of the Wizengamot (Goblet).

The Imperius Curse was used by Barty Crouch Sr. (a ministry official) numerous times at his estate (Goblet).

Theory

The Trace does not work at all for important or high ranking ministry officials. If the Ministry were to fall, the officials would not want themselves or their Aurors to be tracked by location. Because aurors fight dark wizards and remain mysterious, so it makes sense that their magic is not tracked. They were also given permission to use unforgivable curses during the First Wizarding War and after to catch remaining Death Eaters. The Ministry wouldn't want to mistake an Auror for a criminal, then send Aurors at their own Aurors, so they removed the Trace from their Aurors. Dumbledore was reinstated as Chief Warlock of the Wizengamot in mid-1995, and this magic referenced was conducted in late-1995, so because he is a high ranking official in the Ministry, Order of Merlin First Class holder, and Headmaster of Hogwarts, his magic was not tracked. Barty Crouch worked in an extremely high ranking position at the ministry, so if the Ministry could see someone was performing an Imperius Curse in his house every so often, why wouldn't they check out the scene?
